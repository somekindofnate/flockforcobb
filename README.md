# Flock data for Cobb County, Georgia, USA
After fighting with Cobb County for the last 3 months, they have finally produced the following audit records.

A copy of my open records request:

```
Pursuant to the Georgia Open Records Act (O.C.G.A. § 50-18-70 et seq.), I am requesting access to and copies of public records regarding the Cobb County Police Department’s (CCPD) use of the Flock Safety Automated License Plate Recognizer (ALPR) system, specifically concerning data-sharing with federal immigration and customs agencies.

I. Scope of Request
I am requesting all audit logs, query records, and inter-agency data-sharing metadata (excluding the specific captured license plate numbers of private citizens if deemed exempt under § 35-1-22) for the period of February 1, 2025, to February 1, 2026, that reflect searches conducted by or on behalf of:

Homeland Security Investigations (HSI)

Immigration and Customs Enforcement (ICE)

Department of Homeland Security (DHS)

Enforcement and Removal Operations (ERO)

Office of the Principal Legal Advisor (OPLA)

II. Specific Search Terms & Cooperative Activity
This request specifically includes records of queries or "hotlist" entries utilizing terms identified as being used by partner agencies in the region (e.g., Atlanta Police Department) to facilitate immigration enforcement, including but not limited to:

"ICE"

"HSI"

"Immigration"

"Removal"

"ERO"

"Customs"

"Homeland"

The scope includes any communications (emails, memos, or digital logs) between CCPD and the aforementioned federal entities regarding the establishment of "read-only" access or "shared" access to CCPD’s Flock Safety camera network.

III. Legal Justification & O.C.G.A. § 35-1-22 Compliance
While O.C.G.A. § 35-1-22(b) protects captured ALPR data from being "publicly disclosed," this request seeks administrative audit logs and inter-agency communications. Under § 35-1-22(c), law enforcement agencies are required to maintain a record of every person or entity that accesses ALPR data. These access logs are public records that demonstrate the accountability and scope of surveillance.

Furthermore, I reference the recent judicial precedent in Cardinal News v. City of Roanoke (2025), which affirmed that the administrative use of LPR systems—specifically how and when they are queried—is a matter of significant public interest and subject to disclosure.

IV. Format and Costs
I request these records be provided in an electronic format (CSV, Excel, or PDF) to minimize costs. Pursuant to O.C.G.A. § 50-18-71, if the estimated cost for searching and duplicating these records exceeds $50.00, please notify me in advance with an itemized estimate.

If you choose to withhold any portion of these records, please provide a written response within three business days citing the specific statutory exemption for each redaction or withholding, as required by O.C.G.A. § 50-18-71(d).

Thank you for your assistance in this matter.
